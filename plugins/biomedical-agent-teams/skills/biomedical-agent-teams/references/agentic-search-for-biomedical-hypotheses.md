# Agentic Search For Biomedical Hypotheses

Use this reference for idea-discovery and broad research-council workflows that
need more than a simple ranked list.

## Tournament Loop

1. Lock context, entities, privacy boundary, and source scope.
2. Generate a diverse candidate pool.
3. Cluster near-duplicates and merge redundant ideas.
4. Filter for plausibility, novelty, and biological coherence.
5. Run pairwise critique or debate with explicit win/loss rationale.
6. Evolve or recombine candidates only when the mechanism remains testable.
7. Rank by Bayesian expected information gain, feasibility, risk, and fit to
   the user's research program.
8. Red-team the winners for contradictions, confounders, safety, and overclaim.
9. Convert winners into kill-tests and claim-ledger entries.

## Biomedical Ranking Criteria

- Novelty.
- Evidence strength and quality.
- Mechanistic specificity.
- Assayability and feasibility.
- Safety, privacy, dual-use, and translational risk.
- CAR cell therapy or immunology relevance when applicable.
- Expected information gain.
- Ability to falsify the hypothesis with a small, clean experiment.

## Guardrails

- Do not optimize for novelty alone.
- Do not convert public-omics association into cell-intrinsic mechanism without
  direct or appropriately caveated evidence.
- Do not reward post-hoc subgroup discovery without validation.
- Record useful but unverified candidates as excluded/not-ledger-verified
  claims until evidence is checked.
